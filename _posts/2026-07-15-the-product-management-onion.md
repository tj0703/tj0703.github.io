How does the Product Management Onion help Product Owners?

![The Product Management Onion, showing concentric layers from Delivery & Validation at the center out through Value & Solution Discovery, Product Strategy, Product Vision, Business Strategy, to Company Vision at the outermost layer.](/assets/images/product-management-onion.jpg)

Product Owners often find themselves constrained in their ability to fulfill their true accountability due to a lack of authority. These people are commonly called a proxy Product Owner. They may handle day-to-day tasks, but they do not own the product vision or make final decisions. They typically represent someone else who is making those decisions. This situation is unideal and limits the success of a Product Owner.

To progress from a proxy-type Product Owner to a more strategic role that fulfills the true accountability, Product Owners need to move up the layers of the planning onion into value and solution discovery, product strategy, and product vision.

For Product Owners who are not accustomed to operating in this space, embracing the challenge of stepping beyond their comfort zones into more strategic areas requires significant courage.

The first step in this direction is to take a holistic look at the product. This helps a Product Owner see how their product operates within the larger ecosystem of the organization.
